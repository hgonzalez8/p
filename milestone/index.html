Summary

So far, we focused on implementing a functioning volumetric scattering method for our pathtracer. We used distance sampling as our method to determine the distance along the ray sample that intersects with a particle based on the medium's density (i.e, the probability of interaction). We use the Beer-Lambert Law for distance sampling, which calculates the distance travelled by the ray in the medium before colliding with a particle. After distance sampling, we used the Henyey-Greenstein phase function to determine the next direction of the ray after it collides with a particle because it is able to approximate complex light scattering for different scattering media.

Preliminary Results

We have been able to render the image of bunny.dae in three different settings. One with no medium, another with a low density medium, and one with a high density medium. When modeling how the light interacts with the particles. We saw that as the density got higher, the lighting appeared dimmer in the scene. After adding the Henyey-Greenstein phase function and direct lighting, the scene was more visible, the lighting had a glow around it, and we could see the fog throughout the scene.
